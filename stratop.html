Why most strategic plans fail.

Most strategic plans fail for the same three reasons: they're built by too few people, they ignore operational reality, and they have no system for follow-through.

The leadership team flies in for a two-day retreat, fills a whiteboard with ambitions, and goes home with a document nobody fully owns. Within a quarter, the plan is a memory and the whirlwind is back in control.

StratOp is designed to solve exactly that.

"People help build what they help create. That's not a theory — it's a framework for action."

  • Built by too few people — only senior leaders in the room
  • Ignores operational reality — great vision, no execution path
  • No system for follow-through — accountability dies after the retreat

How StratOp works.

StratOp brings the right people into the room and guides them through up to 27 conversations and tools across three phases. Depending on your organization's size, that might include founders, senior leadership, board members, or team leaders.

Phase 1

Preparation & Perspective

Before we ever gather your team, I'll work closely with your appointed leader to shape the agenda. Then I'll conduct one-on-one interviews with every participant. This isn't a formality — it's how I learn where the real tensions are, what's not being said in meetings, and what your team actually needs from this process.

Phase 2

The Experience

Two or three full days together, in person, at your location. We'll work through the strategic, operational, and financial dimensions of your business in a way that doesn't just produce a plan — it produces buy-in. People help build what they help create. That's by design.

Phase 3

Follow-Through

This is where most consultants disappear. We don't. After the experience, I'll train your team on a digital platform to track progress, hold a private session with senior leadership, and check in at 2 months and 10 months to make sure the plan is alive — not archived.

What your team walks away with.

This isn't a binder. It's a living system — the strategic clarity, management tools, and accountability structures your team will actually use long after our work together is done.

  • A full suite of management tools you'll use long after our work together
