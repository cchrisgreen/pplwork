Sound familiar?

You've done the offsite. You've filled the whiteboard. You've built the deck. Six months later, the plan is collecting dust and your team is back to putting out fires.

It's not because the ideas were bad. It's because the plan lacked three things:

Honest perspective

A clear-eyed view of where you actually are — not the investor version, not the board version.

Genuine alignment

Real buy-in from the people who have to execute — not forced agreement after the fact.

A rhythm of accountability

A system that keeps the plan alive — check-ins, milestones, cadences — so execution becomes a habit.

When a plan doesn't have those elements, it never leaves the paper.

What working together looks like.

I don't show up with a pre-built strategy. I show up with the right questions — and a process that's been pressure-tested for over 50 years.

We start with perspective, not answers.

Before we plan anything, we get honest about where you are. Not the investor version. Not the board version. The real version. I'll help your team see your current reality with fresh eyes — the opportunities hiding in plain sight and the assumptions that are quietly costing you.

We align the people, not just the plan.

A strategy only works if the people behind it believe in it. We'll build the plan together — your context, your team's input, your vision. No cookie-cutter frameworks. By the time we're done, your team won't need to be sold on the plan because they helped create it.

We build the rhythm that keeps it moving.

The best plan in the world dies without follow-through. We'll put accountability structures in place — check-ins, milestones, cadences — so execution becomes a habit instead of a hope.

Two ways we work together.
